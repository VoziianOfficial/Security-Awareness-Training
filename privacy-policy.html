01
Introduction

About this Privacy Policy

SecureHabit is a cybersecurity-awareness information and inquiry platform. The website provides educational materials about topics such as phishing, password security, email security, remote work, social engineering and data protection.

This Privacy Policy describes how information may be handled when you visit the website, interact with its features, contact SecureHabit or request information about possible training options.

Do not submit sensitive security material

Do not send passwords, authentication codes, credentials, confidential incident evidence, government identification numbers, payment-card details, health information or sensitive workplace documents through the website.

02
Scope

Where this policy applies

This policy applies to information handled through the SecureHabit website, its contact form, direct email inquiries and website preferences stored in the browser.

It does not automatically apply to websites, tools, platforms or services operated by unrelated third parties. Those services may have their own privacy policies and terms.

Where an inquiry results in direct communication with an independent trainer, consultant, technology provider or cybersecurity specialist, that party may process information under its own privacy practices.

03
Information collected

Categories of information

Information you provide

When you submit a contact or training inquiry, the information may include:

  • Your name and email address.
  • Organization name and professional role.
  • Inquiry type and selected training topic.
  • Approximate team size and preferred timing.
  • Preferred learning or delivery formats.
  • Information included in your message.

Information collected automatically

Depending on the website configuration and services in use, technical information may include:

  • Browser type, device type and operating system.
  • Approximate location derived from an internet protocol address.
  • Pages viewed, referral source and general interaction data.
  • Date, time and duration of a website visit.
  • Error, performance and security logs.

Browser-stored preferences

The website may store limited preferences in your browser, such as whether the cookie notice was acknowledged or which optional preferences were selected.

Data minimization

Please provide only the information reasonably necessary to explain your inquiry.

04
Use of information

Why information may be processed

Information may be used for purposes including:

  • Reviewing and responding to your inquiry.
  • Understanding the audience, topic and format being considered.
  • Providing requested educational information or resources.
  • Assessing whether an inquiry may be appropriate for an independent specialist connection.
  • Operating, maintaining and improving the website.
  • Detecting spam, abuse, security problems or unauthorized activity.
  • Maintaining records of communications and consent.
  • Complying with applicable legal or regulatory obligations.
  • Establishing, exercising or defending legal claims.

Information submitted for one purpose will not intentionally be used for an incompatible purpose without an appropriate reason or notice.

05
Processing grounds

Legal reasons that may support processing

Where applicable law requires a legal basis for processing personal information, the relevant basis may include:

Requested steps

Processing information to respond to a request or take steps connected with a possible service discussion.

Legitimate interests

Operating the website, responding to inquiries, improving resources and protecting the platform from misuse.

Consent

Processing based on a choice you make, where consent is required and can be withdrawn.

Legal obligations

Processing needed to comply with applicable law, lawful requests or recordkeeping requirements.

The available legal basis depends on the information, purpose, location and circumstances involved.

06
Information sharing

When information may be disclosed

Information may be shared only where reasonably necessary for purposes described in this policy, including with:

  • Hosting, infrastructure and website-maintenance providers.
  • Email, communications and inquiry-management providers.
  • Analytics, performance, security or spam-prevention services when enabled.
  • Professional advisers such as legal, accounting or compliance advisers.
  • Government authorities or other parties where disclosure is legally required.
  • A buyer, successor or relevant adviser in connection with a proposed business reorganization, financing, sale or transfer.

Independent specialists

Where appropriate and available, inquiry information may be shared with an independent training or cybersecurity specialist for the purpose of reviewing a possible connection.

Only information reasonably relevant to the inquiry should be shared. A connection is not guaranteed, and any specialist may have separate privacy practices, terms and responsibilities.

Independent provider relationship

SecureHabit does not automatically control how an independent specialist processes information after a direct professional relationship begins.

09
Retention

How long information may be kept

Information may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to:

  • Respond to and manage an inquiry.
  • Maintain relevant communication records.
  • Protect the website from abuse or security threats.
  • Meet accounting, legal, compliance or dispute-related requirements.
  • Establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

Retention periods may differ based on the information, purpose, applicable law and whether an ongoing professional discussion or dispute exists.

When information is no longer reasonably required, it may be deleted, anonymized or securely archived, subject to technical and legal limitations.

Security

Measures intended to protect information

Reasonable technical and organizational measures may be used to reduce risks such as unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, loss or misuse.

Measures may include access controls, account security, software maintenance, secure communications, record-limitation practices and service-provider review.

No transmission method is completely secure

Internet communication and electronic storage cannot be guaranteed to be risk-free. Submit only the information necessary for the inquiry.

If you believe information has been submitted or exposed incorrectly, contact SecureHabit promptly using the details in this policy.

Your choices and rights

Requests concerning personal information

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights concerning personal information, such as:

  • Requesting access to certain personal information.
  • Asking for inaccurate information to be corrected.
  • Requesting deletion in certain circumstances.
  • Requesting restriction of certain processing.
  • Objecting to processing based on particular grounds.
  • Requesting a portable copy where applicable.
  • Withdrawing consent where processing depends on consent.
  • Submitting a complaint to an appropriate data protection authority.

These rights are not absolute. A request may be limited where an exception applies, identity cannot be reasonably verified or retention is required by law.

Submitting a request

Contact SecureHabit using the email address below. Include enough information to identify the relevant communication or inquiry, but do not send identity documents unless they are specifically and securely requested.
