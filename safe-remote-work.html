Work continues beyond the office

The location changes. The security decisions continue.

Review the device, connection, surroundings and information before beginning work.

Remote work can take place at home, in a shared office, during travel or anywhere an approved work arrangement permits.

Each environment introduces different considerations: who can see the screen, who can hear a call, which network is available, where documents are stored and whether the device remains under the employee’s control.

A repeatable review habit can help employees assess the environment before accessing sensitive systems or information.

Public shared space

Working from a café

An employee plans to review customer records and join a confidential meeting from a crowded café.

Review question Is this environment appropriate for the information and conversation involved?

Consider

  • Screen visibility
  • Nearby conversations
  • Connection method
  • Device control

Safer response

Move to an appropriate private environment or postpone sensitive work until organizational privacy and connectivity requirements can be met.

Travel connectivity

Hotel network sign-in

A traveler sees several similarly named wireless networks and an unfamiliar browser page requesting workplace credentials.

Review question Does the connection route match the employer’s approved travel and remote-access process?

Consider

  • Network name verification
  • Unexpected credential request
  • Approved remote-access tool
  • Alternative connectivity

Safer response

Do not enter workplace credentials into the unfamiliar network page. Follow approved travel connectivity or support guidance.

Device control

Device left unattended

An employee plans to leave a company laptop on a shared desk while stepping away for a short time.

Review question Can someone else access, move or view the device while the employee is away?

Consider

  • Screen lock
  • Physical possession
  • Visible documents
  • Shared-space rules

Safer response

Lock and secure the device according to workplace policy rather than leaving it exposed in a shared environment.

Household access

Family member requests the laptop

A household member asks to use the company laptop briefly for a personal task.

Review question Does organizational policy allow another person to use the device, applications or account session?

Consider

  • Device authorization
  • Account-session exposure
  • Workplace applications
  • Information stored locally

Safer response

Keep workplace devices and sessions limited to authorized use unless the employer’s policy explicitly states otherwise.

Remote-Work Security Checklist

  1. Use the approved workplace device and applications.
  2. Use the approved remote-access and network route.
  3. Protect the screen from unauthorized viewing.
  4. Protect calls and meetings from being overheard.
  5. Store workplace documents appropriately.
  6. Keep devices under control and lock them when away.
  7. Keep workplace information in approved systems.
  8. Know the internal support and reporting route.

Remote-work security FAQ

Questions about working beyond the office.

These answers provide general educational information. Employer remote-work, travel, device and security instructions should guide actual workplace actions.

Ask About Training Options
Can I work from any location with internet access?

Not necessarily. The location should comply with organizational remote-work rules and be appropriate for the systems, information and conversations involved.

Is my home network automatically suitable for work?

Suitability depends on employer policy and the approved remote-access process. Follow the provided network, router, VPN, device and support guidance.

Can a family member use my company laptop briefly?

Workplace devices, accounts and active sessions should remain limited to authorized use unless the organization explicitly permits otherwise.

What should I do if I cannot use the normal remote-access tool?

Use the approved support route. Avoid installing unapproved tools, changing security settings or moving workplace information into personal services as a workaround.

Is it acceptable to join a confidential meeting from a public place?

The environment must be appropriate for the conversation and organizational privacy requirements. Move to a suitable private space or postpone the discussion when necessary.

What if a workplace device is lost or stolen?

Follow the organization’s device-loss and incident-reporting process promptly. Provide factual information about the device, location and timing.

Does remote-work security training guarantee protection?

No awareness resource can guarantee protection. Training can support better decisions when combined with appropriate devices, technical controls, processes and qualified security support.
