Fictional phishing scenarios

Compare how warning signs appear in different contexts.

Showing scenario 1 of 4: Suspicious invoice.
Payment request

Suspicious invoice

A supplier-style message includes a new invoice and asks the employee to update payment details before the end of the day.

Subject Final invoice — payment details changed

Warning signs

  • Unexpected change to payment details.
  • Urgent deadline discourages normal review.
  • Attachment was not expected.
  • Reply-to address differs from previous messages.

Verify the supplier and payment change through the organization’s approved independent process.

Account access

Account recovery notice

A message says the employee’s password has expired and provides a link to keep the account active.

Subject Password expires in 10 minutes

Warning signs

  • Very short deadline creates pressure.
  • The sign-in link uses an unfamiliar domain.
  • The message requests account credentials.
  • No expected internal support context is provided.

Open the approved account service independently and verify the alert through the normal recovery route.

File sharing

Unexpected shared document

A familiar colleague’s name appears on a document invitation using a new external sharing platform.

Subject Updated project file — review before meeting

Warning signs

  • A new sharing platform is introduced.
  • The document was not expected.
  • The invitation requests workplace credentials.
  • An upcoming meeting adds time pressure.

Contact the colleague through an approved channel and open the normal workspace independently.

Social engineering

Executive impersonation request

A sender claiming to be a senior leader requests confidential information and says not to involve the usual manager.

Subject Confidential request — respond directly

Warning signs

  • Authority is used to discourage questions.
  • The request asks for secrecy.
  • Normal approval routes should be bypassed.
  • Sensitive information is requested unexpectedly.

Verify the identity and request through a known channel without replying directly to the message.

Printable 01

Phishing Review Checklist

A concise six-point review for unexpected messages, links, files and requests.

  1. Review the full sender address.
  2. Check the message context.
  3. Inspect links and attachments.
  4. Review the requested action.
  5. Verify independently.
  6. Follow the reporting process.
