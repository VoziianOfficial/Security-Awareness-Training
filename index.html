Why secure habits matter

Everyday decisions shape workplace security.

Many security concerns begin with ordinary actions: opening an unexpected message, reusing a password, sharing a document or responding to a request that appears urgent.

SecureHabit turns those moments into practical learning. The goal is not to make every employee a cybersecurity specialist. It is to make careful decisions easier to understand, repeat and discuss.

Recognize

Notice unusual urgency, unfamiliar senders, unexpected files, account requests or pressure to bypass a normal process.

Verify

Confirm the request through an approved independent channel rather than relying only on the message that created the concern.

Respond

Follow the organization’s approved process for access, sharing, account recovery or handling sensitive information.

Report

Share suspicious activity through the internal reporting route so the relevant team can review the situation.

Reinforce

Revisit short scenarios and reminders so careful decisions remain familiar rather than becoming a once-a-year exercise.

Unexpected urgency

Pressure can reduce careful review.

A request that demands immediate action should still be checked through the organization’s approved verification route.

SecureHabit checklist Before responding to an unexpected request
  1. Review the full sender address.
  2. Consider whether the request was expected.
  3. Check links before opening them.
  4. Verify unusual requests independently.
  5. Follow the organization’s reporting process.

Internal policy and instructions from your organization take priority over general guidance.

Employee onboarding timeline

Introduce secure habits in manageable stages.

A learning path can begin with essential account habits and continue through short reminders that reflect how the employee’s work develops.

Day

Account basics

Approved sign-in, unique passwords, multi-factor authentication and account-recovery expectations.

Week

Email and phishing

Sender review, suspicious links, attachments, unexpected requests and internal reporting.

Week

Data handling

Access, approved sharing, storage, retention and responsible disposal of company information.

Week

Remote work

Approved devices, shared spaces, screen privacy, updates and company network procedures.

Ongoing

Short reminders

Brief scenarios and discussion prompts reinforce familiar decisions throughout the year.
