Everyday work creates everyday handling decisions

Information should remain protected throughout its useful lifecycle.

Consider the information, purpose, people and approved system before handling or sharing it.

Workplace information can appear in applications, documents, messages, reports, recordings, exports, screenshots and conversations.

Protecting it is not limited to preventing external access. Employees also make decisions about how much information is needed, who should receive it, where it should be stored and when it should be removed.

A repeatable handling habit can support better decisions without expecting employees to become privacy, legal or information-security specialists.

  3. Use: keep the information within its approved business purpose.

Information appears in many forms

Handle the content according to its purpose and workplace rules.

Classification names and handling requirements differ between organizations. Employees should follow the labels, systems and procedures used by their workplace.

01
Customer and contact information

Information connected to customers, users or contacts

This can include names, contact details, account records, communications, service history or support information.

Use only the information required for the approved workplace purpose.

02 Employee information

Workplace records about employees and applicants

Access should remain limited to appropriate roles, systems and authorized business purposes.

A colleague relationship does not automatically create a need for access.

03 Commercial information

Contracts, pricing, plans and internal decisions

Business information may require controlled access even when it does not contain personal details.

Keep drafts, exports and negotiations in approved collaboration systems.

04 Access information

Credentials, codes, tokens and account-recovery details

Access information should not be copied into documents, chats or unofficial storage unless policy specifically permits it.

Unexpected requests for authentication information should be verified independently.

05 Technical information

System details, configurations, logs and source material

Technical content may expose internal systems, architecture, operational details or security controls.

Confirm the recipient, minimum scope and approved transfer route.

06 Physical information

Printed documents, notes, labels and removable media

Information remains sensitive when it moves from a digital system onto paper or portable media.

Store, transport and dispose of physical material according to workplace policy.

Data-protection basics FAQ

Questions about everyday information handling.

These answers provide general educational guidance. Employer privacy, legal, retention, security and information-classification instructions should guide actual workplace actions.

Ask About Training Options
01 Does data protection apply only to personal information?

No. Organizations may also protect commercial, technical, operational, financial and other internal information according to their policies and legal obligations.

02 Can I send workplace information to my personal email?

Personal email should not become an unofficial workplace transfer or storage route. Use approved systems and follow organizational policy.

03 Is it safe to share a file when the recipient works for the same company?

Internal employment does not automatically create a need for access. Confirm the person, role, purpose, permissions and minimum required information.

04 Can screenshots contain sensitive information?

Yes. Screenshots may capture names, account details, conversations, system information, browser tabs or unrelated records. Review the complete image before sharing it.

05 What should I do if I sent information to the wrong person?

Use the organization’s approved reporting process promptly. Explain what was sent, to whom, when and through which system.

06 Can I paste workplace information into an external online tool?

Only use tools approved for the information and purpose involved. Follow organizational guidance for AI, translation, file-conversion and other external services.

07 Does awareness training guarantee legal or regulatory compliance?

No. Awareness training does not replace legal, privacy, compliance or security advice. It can support better workplace habits alongside appropriate policies, controls and qualified specialists.
